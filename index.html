What is Studio Ghibli?

Studio Ghibli is a Japanese animation studio headquartered in Koganei, Tokyo. It is best known for its animated feature films, and has also produced several short subjects, television commercials, and two television films. Its mascot and most recognizable symbol is a character named Totoro, a giant spirit inspired by raccoon dogs and cats from the 1988 anime film My Neighbor Totoro.

Why is animation so popular in Japan?

The storylines and characters are real, and so are their problems. And because of its visual freedom, producers can make these narratives seem larger than life. In the words of a famous anime expert, Takamasa Sakurai: the unique genre is loved due to its unconventional nature.
